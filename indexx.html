How many languages are still spoken today?
Instruction in the indigenous languages is limited to the 11 government-sponsored languages: Akuapem Twi, Asanti Twi, Fanti, Nzema (Akan ethnic languages), Dagaare and Dagbane (Mole-Dagbani ethnic languages), Ewe, Dangme, Ga, Gonja, and Kasem These languages are recognized by the Ghanaian government and are used in various official contexts, such as:
- Education: They are used as languages of instruction in schools, particularly in the northern regions.
- Media: They are used in radio and TV broadcasts, as well as in print media.
- Government: They are used in official communications, such as public announcements and documents.
- Law: They are used in court proceedings, particularly in cases where the parties involved speak these languages.
Additionally, there are many other local languages spoken in Ghana, with over 100 languages spoken across the country. However, these 11 languages are the ones that are officially recognized and promoted by the government.
